Compare Design Directions

Evaluate each direction against TalkTerm's core experience principles and emotional goals.

1. Centered Stage

  • Simplest layout — maps directly to game-dialog mental model
  • Avatar is the clear focal point
  • Vertical flow: avatar → caption → cards → input
  • Easy to implement and maintain for solo developer
  • Cards limited to horizontal row (3-4 max visible)

2. Theater Wide

  • Horizontal card rail supports 5+ options with scroll
  • Full-width stage creates cinematic presence
  • Good for workflows with many technique/option choices
  • Cards may feel small on narrow windows
  • Slightly more complex responsive behavior

3. Side Panel

  • Avatar always fully visible — never obscured by content
  • Panel gives vertical space for content-heavy cards
  • Natural for output review (scrollable panel)
  • Breaks the centered theater metaphor
  • Feels more like a productivity tool than a companion

4. Immersive

  • Maximum emotional impact — avatar with ambient glow
  • Semi-transparent cards create depth
  • Strongest "wow factor" for first impression
  • May feel heavy for extended work sessions
  • Glow effects need careful performance tuning

5. Compact Professional

  • Top bar with mini avatar maximizes content area
  • Session info visible at all times
  • Best for technical users (Marcus persona)
  • Reduces avatar emotional presence significantly
  • Feels more like a tool than a companion

6. Output Focus

  • Optimized for deliverable review moment
  • Large output panel with structured content
  • Avatar present but secondary to output
  • Great for the "completion" phase of workflows
  • Less suitable for the conversation/decision phases

7. Floating Cards

  • 3D perspective tilt adds tactile, game-like feel
  • Cards feel physical — supports gamified decisions
  • Clean centered layout with added visual depth
  • Perspective effects need GPU — matches Rive requirement
  • Most aligned with "gamified decisions" principle

Recommendation

  • Primary layout: Direction 1 (Centered Stage) — for conversation and decision phases
  • With elements from D7 (Floating Cards) — subtle perspective tilt on card hover for gamified feel
  • And D6 (Output Focus) — avatar shrinks when output panel expands for deliverable review
  • This hybrid adapts layout to the current phase of the workflow while keeping the centered stage as home base
